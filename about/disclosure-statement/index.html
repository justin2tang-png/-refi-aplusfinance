Licensing information

Nature and scope of my advice

Becky can help clients choose and apply for a loan that is suitable for their purpose from a panel of lenders. Once a lender and loan terms are chosen, she can help clients obtain an approval and may also help maintain a loan, for example by assisting with re-fixing.

The financial products Becky can advise on are home loans, investment loans, and construction loans.

Referral partners and other services

Becky can help clients access other services through referral partners where this may make the process easier. She is not able to give advice on the products those referral partners offer, and has not checked whether they can meet a client's specific needs. Clients are free to use other providers or undertake their own research.

Referral partners may include Tower F&G, Initio F&G, Asset Finance, Koura Wealth, UK Pension Transfers, XE Money, Heartland Bank, and Avanti Referrer. Becky is unable to offer legal or tax advice and recommends clients consult a solicitor or accountant for that advice.

Fees and expenses

Generally, clients will not be charged fees for the financial advice provided. This is usually possible because, on settlement of a loan, the lender pays commission to Becky. Exceptions may apply where there is no commission, or where commission must be repaid because a loan is fully or partially repaid or materially changed within the relevant clawback period.

If a one-off fee applies, it would be agreed and authorised in writing before services are completed. For commission clawback, the fee is generally no more than $3,000 plus GST, if any, calculated at $250 plus GST per hour, and will not exceed the amount of commission that needs to be repaid to the lender. Clients will be invoiced and given 30 days to pay.

Some lenders may charge an adviser fee that is capitalised into the loan. Refer to the PDF for the full lender fee tables.

Commissions and incentives

On settlement of a loan, a lender usually provides Becky with a commission payment. This may include upfront commission, ongoing commission, and fixed-rate roll-over fees if Becky assists with refixing. Becky may also receive referral fees, commission payments, incentives, or rewards from lenders or referral partners.

Conflicts of interest are managed by following an advice process focused on client needs and goals, ensuring loan amounts match identified needs, providing commission and fee tables, and undertaking regular training on managing conflicts of interest.

Other conflicts of interest

The disclosure states there are no current conflicts of interest, except that a fee may be paid if Becky facilitates or refers a client to Koura Wealth Limited and the client joins the Koura KiwiSaver Scheme. NZ Financial Services Group Limited holds a partial ownership in Koura Wealth Limited and may benefit from growth in that scheme.

Privacy policy and security

Personal information is collected in accordance with the Privacy Policy. Client confidentiality is important and confidential information will not be disclosed except in accordance with the Privacy Policy.

The Privacy Policy outlines that personal information may be shared with NZ Financial Services Group Limited for audit purposes, to ensure services are delivered in clients' best interests and comply with current regulations. The electronic platform used to store personal information is secure and runs on Amazon Web Services.

Complaints process

If you have a complaint about Becky's financial advice or service, contact Becky by phone or email using the subject line "Complaint - Your Name". Set out the nature of the complaint and the resolution you are seeking. Receipt is aimed to be acknowledged within 24 hours, with an answer aimed within 7 working days.

If a resolution cannot be agreed, complaints can be referred to Financial Services Complaints Limited (FSCL), A Financial Ombudsman Service. Email: complaints@fscl.org.nz. Phone: 0800 347 257. Website: https://www.fscl.org.nz/. Postal address: PO Box 5967, Wellington 6140.

My duties

Becky is bound by duties under the Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013, including meeting competence, knowledge, and skill standards in the Code of Professional Conduct for Financial Advice Services; giving priority to client interests; exercising care, diligence, and skill; and meeting standards of ethical behaviour, conduct, and client care.
