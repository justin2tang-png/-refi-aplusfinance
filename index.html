There are some costs involved: break fees on your current loan, valuation fees (~$500-800), and solicitor fees (~$800-1,200). The good news is that many banks offer cashback of $2,000-4,000 that often covers these costs — so it can be cost-neutral or even profitable to switch.
A credit check is part of the application process, which may have a small temporary impact. However, in New Zealand, multiple credit enquiries within a 30-day window count as a single check, so shopping around won’t hurt your score.
This depends on your loan amount and the rate difference. A typical saving is $200-500 per month on a $500,000 loan with a 0.5-1% rate drop. Use our calculator above for a personalised estimate.
Most refinances are completed within 2-3 weeks from your initial application. The process is mostly online, and we handle the coordination between banks and solicitors.
No deposit is needed. Refinancing uses your existing home equity. You generally need at least 20% equity in your home, though some lenders may accept less depending on your situation.
Lenders assess your ability to service the loan at a test rate (usually 1.5-2% above the actual rate). Each lender has different criteria, and we can help match you with the right one based on your income and expenses.
