When Cashflow Lending Fits — Common Triggers and Scenarios

何时适合现金流融资——常见触发因素与场景

Cashflow lending is a form of finance secured against a business's projected revenue rather than its physical assets. It can suit established New Zealand businesses with consistent income streams — but it is not available in every situation.

现金流融资是一种以企业预期收入而非实物资产作为担保的融资形式。它适合具有稳定收入来源的成熟新西兰企业——但并非适用于所有情况。

Common triggers for exploring cashflow lending include:

考虑现金流融资的常见触发因素包括：

- Seasonal working capital gaps. A business may generate 70% of its annual revenue in four months but need to cover rent, wages, and supplier payments year-round. Cashflow lending can smooth these troughs. |- Stocking for a major order. A retailer or wholesaler awarded a large contract may need to purchase inventory 60–90 days before being paid. A cashflow facility can fund that upfront cost. |- Short-term equipment hire rather than purchase. If a business needs a specialist machine for a specific project, a cashflow-backed hire arrangement may be more practical than a term loan. |- Covering an unexpected expense. A critical piece of machinery breaks down, or a key supplier demands shorter payment terms. Cashflow lending can provide bridge funding while the business adjusts.

- 季节性营运资金缺口。企业可能在四个月内创造全年70%的收入，但需要全年支付租金、工资和供应商款项。现金流融资可以平滑这些低谷期。 |- 为大订单备货。零售商或批发商获得大额合同后，可能需要在收款前60–90天采购库存。现金流融资可以垫付前期成本。 |- 短期设备租赁而非购买。如果企业为特定项目需要专用设备，现金流支持的租赁安排可能比定期贷款更实际。 |- 应对突发支出。关键设备故障，或主要供应商要求缩短付款周期。现金流融资可在企业调整期间提供过桥资金。

Lenders typically look for:

贷款机构通常关注：

- At least 12–24 months of trading history with stable or growing revenue. |- A clear, repeatable revenue model — subscription, retainer, or regular purchase cycles are viewed more favourably than one-off project income. |- Profitability or a clear path to it. Lenders may accept modest losses in a growth phase, but sustained negative cashflow is a concern. |- A debt-service coverage ratio (DSCR) of 1.2x or higher on proposed repayments.

- 至少12–24个月的经营历史，且收入稳定或持续增长。 |- 清晰、可复制的收入模式——订阅制、预付款制或定期采购周期比一次性项目收入更受青睐。 |- 盈利或明确的盈利路径。贷款机构可接受增长阶段的适度亏损，但持续负现金流是问题。 |- 建议还款的偿债覆盖率（DSCR）达到1.2倍或以上。

Cashflow lending is generally not suitable for:

现金流融资通常不适用于：

- Startups with no trading history. |- Businesses with highly irregular or unpredictable revenue. |- Ventures where the borrowing need exceeds 50–60% of projected annual cashflow.

- 没有经营历史的新创企业。 |- 收入极不规则或难以预测的企业。 |- 借款需求超过预期年现金流50–60%的业务。

Documents for a Cashflow Lending Application

现金流融资申请所需文件

Preparing a complete application package can reduce delays and improve the likelihood of approval. While each lender's requirements vary, the core documents expected for a cashflow lending application in New Zealand are broadly consistent.

准备完整的申请材料可以减少延误并提高获批可能性。虽然各贷款机构要求有所不同，但新西兰现金流融资申请的核心文件大致相同。

Financial statements:

财务报表：

- At least two years of annual financial statements (profit and loss, balance sheet, notes). If your business is less than two years old, expect to provide all available statements plus a detailed forecast. |- Interim management accounts dated within 90 days of the application. |- A projected cashflow statement covering at least 12 months, with clear assumptions about revenue growth, cost increases, and capital expenditure.

- 至少两年的年度财务报表（损益表、资产负债表、附注）。如果企业成立不足两年，需提供所有可用报表及详细预测。 |- 申请日期90天内的中期管理账目。 |- 至少覆盖12个月的预计现金流量表，需明确收入增长、成本增加和资本支出的假设条件。

Bank statements:

银行流水：

- 6–12 months of business bank statements for the primary operating account. |- Lenders will review the consistency of deposits, the pattern of withdrawals, and any overdraft usage. |- If you use multiple accounts (e.g., separate GST, savings, or payroll accounts), statements for all of them may be required.

- 主要经营账户6–12个月的企业银行流水。 |- 贷款机构会审查存款的稳定性、取款模式以及透支使用情况。 |- 如果使用多个账户（如单独的GST、储蓄或工资账户），可能需要提供全部账户的流水。

Revenue documentation:

收入证明文件：

- A schedule of your top 5–10 customers and the percentage of revenue each represents. Lenders want to see diversification — a single customer accounting for 40%+ of revenue is a concentration risk. |- Copies of key contracts or recurring invoices, especially if the lending is tied to an invoice-financing facility. |- For subscription or retainer models, a churn report showing customer retention over the past 12 months.

- 前5–10大客户清单及各客户收入占比。贷款机构希望看到收入多元化——单一客户占比超过40%属于集中风险。 |- 关键合同或经常性发票的副本，特别是当贷款与发票融资挂钩时。 |- 对于订阅制或预付款制模式，提供显示过去12个月客户留存率的流失报告。

Business and personal information:

企业及个人信息：

- Company registration documents (NZBN, Certificate of Incorporation). |- Personal financial statements for directors or guarantors who own 20% or more of the business. |- A brief business plan or one-page summary explaining the purpose of the loan, the repayment strategy, and how the funding will improve the business's position.

- 公司注册文件（新西兰商业编号、公司注册证书）。 |- 持股20%及以上的董事或担保人的个人财务报表。 |- 简要商业计划或一页摘要，说明贷款用途、还款策略以及资金将如何改善企业状况。

Additional documents if applicable:

其他适用文件：

- GST returns for the last 6–8 periods (lenders often cross-check these against bank deposits). |- IRD tax certificates to confirm the business is up to date with tax obligations. |- If the facility involves an existing debt refinance, a current loan statement and payout figure.

- 最近6–8期的GST申报表（贷款机构常将其与银行入账进行交叉核对）。 |- IRD税务证明，确认企业税务义务已及时履行。 |- 如果融资涉及现有债务再融资，需提供当前贷款对账单和清偿金额。

Invoice Financing vs Term Lending — What's the Difference?

发票融资与定期贷款——有何不同？

Invoice financing and term lending are both forms of business credit, but they serve different purposes and suit different situations. Understanding the distinction can help you choose the right tool for your business.

发票融资和定期贷款都是企业信贷形式，但用途不同，适合的情况也不同。了解两者的区别可以帮助您为企业选择正确的工具。

Invoice financing (also called debtor finance or factoring):

发票融资（也称为应收账款融资或保理）：

- The lender advances funds against outstanding invoices that your customers are yet to pay. Typical advance rates are 70–85% of the invoice value. |- Repayment happens when your customer settles the invoice — usually within 30–60 days. The facility is ongoing and revolving, not a one-off drawdown. |- Interest is calculated daily on the drawn amount, and there is often a service or administration fee. Because the term is short, the total dollar cost can be lower than a term loan for the same principal. |- Best suited for: businesses with creditworthy customers who pay on standard terms (30–60 days), and where the main working capital constraint is the gap between delivering goods or services and receiving payment. |- Less suited for: businesses with long payment cycles (90+ days), high customer concentration, or a small number of very large invoices.

- 贷款机构根据客户尚未支付的应收账款预支资金。典型的预支比例为发票金额的70–85%。 |- 当您的客户结清发票时完成还款——通常为30–60天。该额度是持续循环的，而非一次性放款。 |- 利息按已提取金额每日计算，通常还有服务费或管理费。由于期限短，相同本金的总体美元成本可能低于定期贷款。 |- 最适合：客户信誉良好且按标准期限（30–60天）付款的企业，主要营运资金瓶颈在于交付商品或服务与收到付款之间的时间差。 |- 不太适合：付款周期长（90天以上）、客户集中度高或仅有少量大额发票的企业。

Term lending:

定期贷款：

- A lump sum is advanced and repaid over a fixed schedule — commonly 1–5 years for business loans, though longer terms may be available for asset-backed lending. |- Repayments are typically monthly and include principal and interest, so the balance reduces predictably over time. |- The interest rate is usually fixed or floating for the loan term, providing certainty of repayments. |- Best suited for: a defined, one-off purpose — purchasing equipment, funding a renovation, buying out a partner, or any situation where the business needs capital for longer than an invoice cycle. |- Less suited for: covering recurring, seasonal, or unpredictable cashflow gaps, where the business would pay interest on undrawn funds.

- 一次性放款，按固定时间表还款——企业贷款通常为1–5年，资产支持贷款可能有更长期限。 |- 还款通常为按月分期，包含本金和利息，余额随时间可预测地减少。 |- 利率通常为固定或浮动利率，提供还款的可预见性。 |- 最适合：明确的、一次性用途——购买设备、装修、买断合伙人股份，或任何企业需要资金超过一个发票周期的情况。 |- 不太适合：覆盖经常性、季节性或不明确的现金流缺口，因为企业将为未提取的资金支付利息。

Key differences at a glance:

核心差异一览：

- Purpose: Invoice financing addresses timing gaps in cashflow; term lending funds a specific capital need. |- Duration: Invoice facilities are revolving and short-term; term loans have a fixed end date. |- Cost: Invoice financing can be cheaper in dollar terms for short periods; term loans offer predictable costs over the life of the loan. |- Security: Invoice financing is secured against the ledger; term loans may be secured against business assets or a director's personal guarantee.

- 用途：发票融资解决现金流的时间差问题；定期贷款满足特定的资本需求。 |- 期限：发票融资是循环短期额度；定期贷款有固定的到期日。 |- 成本：发票融资在短期内的美元成本可能更低；定期贷款在整个贷款期间提供可预测的成本。 |- 担保：发票融资以应收账款账册为担保；定期贷款可能以企业资产或董事个人担保为抵押。
